UNIVERSITY OF CHICAGO

CONSENT FORM FOR RESEARCH PARTICIPATION

 

Study Title: Iterated Creative Gesture

Principal Investigator: Susan Goldin-Meadow

Researcher(s): Monica Do

IRB Study Number: 97-074

 

You are being asked to participate in this research study conducted because you expressed interest in participating. You were selected as a possible participant because you are a native speaker of English, aged 18 or older, with no known learning, visual or reading disabilities. Your participation is voluntary and you may choose to stop participating at any time. You should read the information below, and ask questions about anything you do not understand, before deciding whether or not to participate.

 

PURPOSE OF THE STUDY. The aim of the study is to further our understanding of how people comprehend, use and process language.

 

PROCEDURES. If you volunteer to participate in this study, you will watch a series of short videos (e.g., a woman putting on a ring) and describe each of those videos. Rather than describing what you saw in the videos with speech, you describe what you saw using only gestures. For this study, you may be asked to work as an individual or with a partner. The total length of participation would be approximately 5 minutes.

 

POTENTIAL RISKS AND DISCOMFORTS. There are no known risks associated with this kind of study. As with all research, there is a chance that confidentiality of the information we collect from you could be breached â€“ we will take steps to minimize this risk, as discussed in more detail below in this form.

 

POTENTIAL BENEFITS TO SUBJECTS AND/OR TO SOCIETY. There is no direct benefit that you can expect to receive as a result of participating in this study. The data we collect will help us to understand more about how language develops in society.

 

INCENTIVES. You will be paid the amount advertised for your participation. Prolific does not allow for prorated compensation. In the event of an incomplete HIT, you must contact the research team and compensation will be determined based on what was completed and the researchers' discretion. Please note this study contains attention checks to make sure that participants are finishing the tasks honestly and completely. As long as you read the instructions and complete the tasks, your HIT will be approved. If you fail these checks, your HIT will be rejected.

 

CONFIDENTIALITY. Your study data will be handled as confidentially as possible. Your Prolific ID will be used to distribute payment to you but will not be stored with the research data we collect from you. We will not be accessing any personally identifying information about you that you may have put on your Prolific profile page.

In order to see your gestures, we will need to video-record your responses. This is required for participation. These videos will be kept on a secure server that will only be accessible by members of the research team and will only be used for research purposes.

The results of this study may be used in publications/presentations or shared with others for use in future research. If we share or present the data that we collect about you, we will remove any information that could identify you before we share it. This means that individual names and other personally identifiable information will not be used.

At the end of this form we will ask you whether we may use these videos in future studies and/or shown to other participants.

 

PARTICIPANT RIGHTS. Participation in this study is voluntary. You do not have to answer any question you do not want to answer. You may withdraw from this study at any time, and you will not be penalized in any way for deciding to stop participation. If you decide to withdraw from this study, any data already collected will be destroyed.

 

CONTACTS & QUESTIONS. If you have any questions or concerns about the research, please feel free to contact Dr. Monica Do via email at monicado@uchicago.edu. If you have any questions about your rights as a participant in this research, feel you have been harmed, or wish to discuss other study-related concerns with someone who is not part of the research team, you can contact the University of Chicago Social & Behavioral Sciences Institutional Review Board (IRB) Office by phone at (773) 702-2915, or by email at sbs-irb@uchicago.edu.
